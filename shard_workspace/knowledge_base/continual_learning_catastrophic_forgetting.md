## Key Concepts
* Catastrophic forgetting: a phenomenon where neural networks quickly overwrite previously learned knowledge when accommodating new tasks.
* Continual learning: a paradigm where models learn from a stream of tasks without forgetting previously learned knowledge.
* EWC regularization: a technique that mitigates catastrophic forgetting by balancing plasticity and stability in neural networks.
* Balancing plasticity and stability: crucial for preventing catastrophic forgetting and improving robustness in neural networks.

## Pro & Contro
| Pro | Contro |
|-----|--------|
| Improves robustness and adaptability of neural networks | Increases complexity of the learning process |
| Enables learning from a stream of tasks | Requires careful tuning of hyperparameters |
| Mitigates catastrophic forgetting | Can be computationally expensive |
