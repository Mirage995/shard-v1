## Key Concepts

- **Impossible Differential Cryptanalysis**: A cryptanalytic technique that exploits differentials that cannot occur with probability greater than zero to eliminate wrong key guesses
- **Differential Characteristics**: Patterns of input/output differences through cipher rounds that propagate with specific probabilities
- **Exception Handling Boundaries**: Points in cipher execution where impossible differentials create contradictions, allowing key material elimination
- **Subkey Recovery**: Process of extracting round key material by analyzing which keys could not have produced observed ciphertext pairs
- **Bijective Round Functions**: One-to-one mappings in block ciphers that guarantee certain differential paths are impossible
- **Miss-in-the-Middle**: Technique where differential paths from encryption and decryption directions meet at a contradiction point
- **Key Sieving**: Filtering process that eliminates candidate subkeys inconsistent with impossible differential properties
